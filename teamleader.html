Team Leader - Engineering Design Project

Engineering Strategies and Practices II

Project Overview

As part of the Engineering Strategies and Practices II course, I led a team of seven engineers in developing a solution to a real-world environmental problem: pollution caused by steel-corroding parking blocks. This project required innovative thinking, effective team management, and close collaboration with a client to deliver a practical, environmentally-friendly solution.

Problem Statement

The client presented a significant environmental challenge: traditional steel parking blocks were corroding over time, releasing harmful pollutants into the environment. Our team was tasked with designing an alternative solution that would:

  • Eliminate Corrosion: Prevent the release of harmful steel corrosion products
  • Maintain Functionality: Preserve the safety and effectiveness of parking blocks
  • Cost-Effective: Provide a viable economic alternative to existing solutions
  • Environmentally Sustainable: Reduce environmental impact throughout the product lifecycle

Leadership Role

Team Management

As the team leader, I was responsible for coordinating a diverse group of seven engineers:

  • Project Coordination: Ensured all project guidelines were strictly followed and deadlines consistently met
  • Communication Facilitation: Fostered effective communication and cooperation among team members
  • Task Distribution: Assigned roles and responsibilities based on individual strengths and expertise
  • Progress Monitoring: Tracked project milestones and ensured timely delivery of deliverables

Client Relations

Maintained strong relationships with the client throughout the project lifecycle:

  • Regular Communication: Scheduled and conducted regular meetings with the client
  • Requirement Gathering: Collected and documented client specifications and constraints
  • Progress Updates: Provided regular updates on project development and milestones
  • Final Presentation: Led the team presentation that thoroughly impressed the client

Technical Implementation

Design Process

Utilized advanced CAD software and engineering principles to develop the solution:

  • Fusion 360 Design: Created detailed 3D models and technical drawings
  • Material Selection: Researched and selected corrosion-resistant materials
  • Prototype Development: Designed and tested multiple prototype iterations
  • Performance Analysis: Conducted thorough testing to validate design effectiveness

Solution Development

The team developed an innovative solution that addressed all client requirements:

  • Alternative Materials: Identified and tested non-corrosive materials
  • Structural Integrity: Ensured the solution maintained required strength and durability
  • Manufacturing Feasibility: Designed for cost-effective production and installation
  • Environmental Impact: Minimized ecological footprint throughout the lifecycle

Project Deliverables

  • Technical Documentation: Comprehensive design specifications and analysis
  • 3D Models: Detailed CAD models and technical drawings
  • Prototype Testing: Validation of design performance and durability
  • Cost Analysis: Economic feasibility study and production estimates
  • Environmental Assessment: Lifecycle analysis and environmental impact evaluation

Leadership Skills Developed

  • Project Management: Planning, organizing, and executing complex engineering projects
  • Team Coordination: Managing diverse team dynamics and ensuring collaboration
  • Communication: Effective verbal and written communication with stakeholders
  • Problem Solving: Systematic approach to complex engineering challenges
  • Client Relations: Building and maintaining professional client relationships
  • Technical Leadership: Guiding technical decisions and ensuring quality standards

Technical Skills Enhanced

  • CAD Software: Advanced proficiency in Fusion 360 for 3D modeling and design
  • Material Science: Understanding of material properties and selection criteria
  • Design Analysis: Structural and performance analysis of engineering solutions
  • Prototyping: Development and testing of physical prototypes
  • Documentation: Technical writing and engineering documentation

Project Outcomes

The project was highly successful, with the client thoroughly impressed by our extensive research and attention to detail. The solution demonstrated:

  • Innovation: Creative approach to solving a complex environmental problem
  • Technical Excellence: Rigorous engineering analysis and validation
  • Client Satisfaction: Exceeded client expectations and requirements
  • Team Success: Effective collaboration and project completion
